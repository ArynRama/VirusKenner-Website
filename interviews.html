Dear students, Please find the answers in the text below Regards, Marja Y van Eer, specialist internal medicine and hiv

Questions for HIV specialist:

What is the best way to protect yourself against HIV?

On the workfloor one should avoid needle accidents and privately, as anyone should, always practice safe sex.

How have you contributed to HIV research?

1992 till 1994 Leiden Clinical research on the effect of immunisation in HIV patients and effects of ART. 2021 and 2022 research on partnernotification and on adolesents with hiv

Have you ever come close to curing a patient?

There is no cure for hiv. ART makes the viral sleep and contributes to good health

What does HIV-treatment look like and does it have any side effects?

There are different combinaions of drugs. The one we use most is a combination of three drugs in one pill. This is tolerated well. Sometimes in the beginning nausea or frequent stool. But most patients do not experience this. Long term effects are depositions of fat on hips,in the abdomen and in the neck region and some times cardiovascular complications.

How can I tell if my HIV treatment is working?

By measurieng the hiv viral load in the blood

Is the treatment affordable for everyone?

In Suriname the governement pays for the treatment. If this was not the case we would hav a lot of persons who would not be able to pay for medication.

What are some of the HIV myths you have come across often?

Attracting hiv by musquitobites or bysitting next to an hiv positive patient.

How can people help raise awareness?

By declaring that hiv seropositives have a chronic condition which should not lead to discrimination and live guided by this principle.

What message regarding HIV do you have for the world?

Stop stigmatizing, practice safe sex, test regularly on hiv as long as your sexual behaviour is risky.
